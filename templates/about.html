1.2 THE AIM AND OBJECTIVES
This research aims to develop a predictive model for evaluating stroke risk and deploy
it on the Heroku cloud platform using Flask, a microweb framework written in Python.
The following are the specific objectives.
1. Identify and acquire suitable datasets containing relevant stroke risk factors and
outcomes.
2. To perform exploratory data analysis and prepare the data for model training.
3. To research and implement machine learning algorithms suitable for stroke risk
prediction, considering factors like interpretability, performance, and scalability.
4. To evaluate the performance of the developed model using appropriate metrics
such as accuracy, precision, recall, and area under the curve (AUC).
5. To design and develop a user-friendly web interface using Flask that allows
users to input their health information and receive personalized stroke risk
predictions.
6. To integrate the developed prediction model into the Flask application, ensuring
seamless functionality and real-time prediction capabilities.
7. To configure and deploy the Flask application on the Heroku cloud platform,
considering security, scalability, and accessibility.
1.3 RESEARCH QUESTIONS
The study will attempt to provide answers to the following research questions.
I. What are the most suitable publicly available datasets that contain relevant
stroke risk factors and outcomes, and that fulfil the ethical and privacy
requirements of this dissertation?
II. What preprocessing steps are required to get the data ready for training
machine learning models, and what insights can be obtained from exploratory
data analysis of the chosen dataset?
III. What machine learning algorithms are most suitable for predicting stroke risk,
considering factors such as interpretability, performance, and scalability to
large datasets?
IV. How well does the chosen model predict stroke risk in terms of accuracy,
precision, recall, and AUC, and is its performance satisfactory for reliable
deployment?
V. What design principles and interface elements will create a user-friendly and
accessible web application for inputting health information and receiving
personalized stroke risk predictions?
VI. How can the developed prediction model be seamlessly integrated into the
Flask application, while ensuring real-time predictions and user-friendly
interaction?
VII. What are the best ways to set up and run a Flask application on the Heroku
cloud platform so that end users can be assured of security, scalability, and
accessibility?
